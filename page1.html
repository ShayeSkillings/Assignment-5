Norman Maclean

Norman Maclean Norman Maclean (1902-1990) was an American author known for his novella "A River Runs Through It." He grew up in Montana, where his love for fly fishing and the outdoors influenced his writing. Maclean taught English at the University of Chicago and published his famous novel in his later years. His work captures the beauty of nature, family dynamics, and the human experience. Maclean's writing continues to inspire readers with its lyrical prose and profound insights into life in the American West.
